# Demand Validation — Interview Guide + Outreach Templates

**Goal:** before writing any code, validate that VN EHS managers will pay 2.49M VND/mo (~$99) for a MOIT-compliant VI safety card generator.

**Target:** 10 conversations in 14 days. ≥5 enthusiastic → green light. <3 enthusiastic → pivot or kill.

## 4. Interview Structure (30 minutes)

```
0:00 – 2:00   Intro + consent to record
2:00 – 8:00   Their current workflow (no mention of our product yet)
8:00 – 15:00  Pain points + cost quantification
15:00 – 22:00 Reveal concept + reaction
22:00 – 27:00 Pricing + decision-maker questions
27:00 – 30:00 Ask for referrals + design partner commitment
```

**Rule:** don't talk about our product in the first 15 minutes. Listen. They'll either describe our solution back to us or not — and that tells you everything.

---

## 5. Full Question Bank

### Section A — Context (minutes 2–8)
1. Tell me about your role. What's your team size? How long have you been doing this?
2. How many unique chemicals does your company handle? How many SDSs in your archive?
3. How often do new SDSs arrive? Where do they come from (suppliers, procurement, new products)?
4. Walk me through exactly what happens from "English SDS arrives in inbox" to "warehouse worker has access to VN safety info". Every step.
5. What tools do you use today? (SharePoint, email, paper, software?)
6. Who else in the company touches this process?

### Section B — Pain + Cost (minutes 8–15)
7. What's the most frustrating part of this workflow?
8. How do you currently produce Vietnamese safety cards / instructions? Do you do it yourself, or outsource?
9. If outsourced: who do you use? What do they charge per SDS? How long does each take?
10. If in-house: how many hours/month does your team spend on this? Who does it?
11. What happens during a MOIT audit? Have you been audited? What did they check? Note: penalties framework updated under Law on Chemicals 2025.
12. Has anything ever gone wrong — a missing card, a wrong translation, an incident?
13. On a scale of 1–10, how painful is this problem?
14. Are you aware of Circular 01/2026/TT-BCT (effective Jan 17, 2026)? How does the new regulation change your compliance workload?

### Section C — Reveal (minutes 15–22)
> *Show screen with the concept: "Upload English SDS → 30 seconds later → MOIT-compliant VN safety card PDF with QR code"*

14. What's your first reaction?
15. What's missing from this picture for you?
16. What would make you trust the AI output?
17. Is the QR-code-to-mobile view useful, or does it add complexity?
18. What would you NOT trust the AI to do?
19. Would you let the AI answer compliance questions ("Is chemical X restricted under VN law?") via chat?

### Section D — Commercial (minutes 22–27)
20. If this existed today at 2.49M VND/month for unlimited cards, would you buy it?
    - If yes: "Would you buy it THIS month if I had it ready?"
    - If no: "What price would work?"
21. Who approves software purchases? What's the process/timeline?
22. What would need to be true for this to become a 10/10 must-have?
23. Have you tried any other software for this? (NextSDS, VelocityEHS, ChemWatch, etc.)

### Section E — Close (minutes 27–30)
24. Would you be a design partner? Weekly 30-min calls for 3 months, in exchange for:
    - Lifetime 50% discount
    - Priority feature requests
    - Co-branded case study (optional)
25. Who else should I talk to? Can you introduce me to 2 people?
26. May I follow up in 2 weeks with progress?

## 6. Red Flags to Watch For

| Flag | Meaning | Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Interesting, but we use [spreadsheet/SharePoint] and it works fine" | Low pain, not a buyer | Thank + exit gracefully, don't push |
| "We have a consultant who handles this" | The consultant IS the buyer/blocker | Ask if you can meet the consultant — they may be a channel partner |
| "MOIT never audits us" | Complacency, low urgency | Low priority lead, deprioritize |
| "We only handle 5 chemicals" | TAM too small for this customer | Wrong ICP, deprioritize |
| "Can you do it in English too, for our Singapore office?" | Expansion signal | Note for post-MVP, keep in loop |
| "How does it integrate with SAP/Oracle?" | Enterprise, not SMB | Outside MVP scope — flag as future enterprise lead |
| "Free version?" | Price-sensitive, low value perception | Probe — might not be ICP, or might be an influencer |
| Vague answers, no specifics | Not actually doing this themselves | Wrong person, ask for referral to real owner |

## 7. Post-Interview Scoring Rubric (0–5 per dimension)

| Dimension | 0 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Problem pain level** | "It's fine" | "It's annoying" | "It's breaking us" |
| **Current spend (VND/mo on this)** | 0 | 2–10M | >10M |
| **SDS volume** | <10 | 20–100 | >100 |
| **Buying authority** | None | Influencer | Direct buyer |
| **Enthusiasm at reveal** | Polite | Interested | "When can I buy?" |
| **Willingness to be design partner** | No | Maybe | Yes, confirmed |
| **Price acceptance at 2.49M** | "Too much" | "Fair" | "Cheap" |
| **Referral quality** | 0 names | 1 name | 2+ warm intros |

**Total scoring:**
- **≥28/40** = enthusiastic prospect → design partner candidate
- **18–27** = warm lead → nurture, re-engage post-MVP
- **<18** = not ICP → thank + exit

## 8. Kill / Pass Criteria (end of 14 days)

### PASS (green light coding)
- ≥5 interviews with total score ≥28
- ≥2 design partner confirmations (including Asia Shine)
- ≥1 prospect says "I'd pay today if it existed"
- ≥5M VND average current EHS consulting spend across respondents

### PIVOT signals
- <3 high scores but consultants say "I'd use this to serve my clients" → B2B2B consultant channel play
- Pain is real but price is wrong → reprice at 499k or 99k/month, retest
- Pain is in a different feature (e.g., expiry alerts over safety cards) → reprioritize MVP

### KILL signals
- <3 interviews reach >28 score
- Nobody currently spends money solving this
- All prospects say "AI scares us, we need a human"
- Decision cycles >6 months everywhere → can't close before runway ends

## 11. Logistics

- **Recording:** Ask consent. Use Zoom cloud recording or Riverside. Transcribe via Whisper.
- **Note-taking:** Don't try to type during interview — record + transcribe later.
- **Timezone:** All in Asia/Saigon. Don't force evening calls on VN respondents.
- **Language:** Vietnamese unless interviewee prefers English. Don't code-switch unless they do first.
- **Dress / setting:** Professional. Background clean. Camera on.
- **Cancellations:** Expect 30% no-show rate. Book 14 to get 10.
- **Gratitude:** Send a thank-you message within 2 hours of each call. Promise updates.
